Comprehensive Coverage for Senior Cruisers

Seniors are one of the largest and fastest-growing cruise demographics in New Zealand — and for good reason. Cruising offers the perfect combination of comfort, variety, and ease of travel. But the right insurance is more important for seniors than any other traveller group.

Why Seniors Need Specialised Cruise Insurance

Medical emergencies are statistically more likely for older travellers, and the consequences of being uninsured are more severe. Many standard travel insurance policies either exclude seniors, charge prohibitive premiums, or have inadequate coverage limits.

Our senior cruise insurance specialists work with providers who:

  • Cover travellers up to age 85+ on many policies
  • Include pre-existing conditions with full disclosure
  • Provide truly unlimited medical coverage
  • Offer repatriation to NZ as a standard benefit

Pre-Existing Medical Conditions

The majority of New Zealanders over 65 have at least one pre-existing medical condition. Our advisors specialise in finding policies that cover your specific conditions — whether that's heart disease, diabetes, respiratory conditions, or others.

**Important:** You must declare all pre-existing conditions. Failing to disclose may void your policy. Our advisors help you navigate the declaration process correctly.

What Seniors Cruise Insurance Covers

Unlimited Medical Expenses

Every senior deserves unlimited medical coverage when cruising — no $50,000 or $100,000 sub-limits.

Emergency Medical Evacuation

Full evacuation costs from ship or port — essential given potentially longer hospitalisation periods.

Medical Repatriation to NZ

Return home for ongoing treatment — particularly important for seniors managing ongoing conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need cruise insurance if I already have travel insurance?
Yes — standard travel insurance typically does not cover the unique risks associated with cruising. You need to either add a cruise extension to your existing policy or purchase dedicated cruise insurance. Standard policies often exclude cabin confinement, miss-port departure, and have inadequate limits for maritime medical evacuations.
Does my NZ health insurance cover me on a cruise?
No. Once your ship departs port — even on domestic NZ cruises — you are in a maritime environment where standard NZ health insurance does not apply. Ship's doctors charge private rates, and onboard medical costs are entirely your responsibility without cruise insurance.
What is cabin confinement cover?
Cabin confinement cover provides a daily cash benefit (typically $100–$250/day) if illness or injury keeps you confined to your cabin. Since you've paid for excursions and activities you can't enjoy, this benefit helps compensate for the experience you're missing out on.
What is missed port departure cover?
If your connecting flight or transport to the cruise embarkation port is delayed or cancelled due to a covered reason, missed port departure cover pays for you to catch up with your ship at the next port — including flights, accommodation, and meals while you wait.
