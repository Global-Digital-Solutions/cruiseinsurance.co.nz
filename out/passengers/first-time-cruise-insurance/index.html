Welcome to Cruise Insurance — A Guide for First Timers

If this is your first cruise, you may be wondering: do I really need cruise insurance? The answer is unequivocally yes — and understanding why will help you choose the right cover.

What Makes Cruise Insurance Different from Regular Travel Insurance

Standard travel insurance policies are designed for land-based holidays. They typically:

  • Exclude or limit onboard medical expenses
  • Don't cover cabin confinement (being bedridden on the ship)
  • Don't cover missed port departures
  • May have inadequate medical evacuation limits for maritime emergencies

Cruise-specific insurance covers all of these scenarios with appropriate limits.

The Costs You Need to Protect Against

Onboard Medical Expenses

The ship's doctor is not free — consultations cost $200–$500, and hospitalisation on a ship can cost $1,500–$5,000/day.

Medical Evacuation

If you need to be airlifted off the ship, costs range from $15,000 (close to port) to $150,000 (international waters).

Trip Cancellation

Cruise bookings are often made months in advance. If you can't travel due to illness, your insurance reimburses non-refundable costs.

Cabin Confinement

If seasickness or illness keeps you in your cabin, you receive a daily benefit for the activities you're missing.

What First-Time Cruise Insurance Covers

Unlimited Medical Expenses

No dollar caps on medical treatment — crucial for maritime medical emergencies.

Emergency Evacuation

Full cost of evacuation from the ship to medical facilities.

Cabin Confinement

Daily benefit (typically $100–$250/day) for time confined to your cabin.

Cancellation & Interruption

Full reimbursement of non-refundable cruise costs.

24/7 Emergency Assistance

Round-the-clock support in any situation — especially valuable for first-time cruisers unfamiliar with onboard procedures.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need cruise insurance if I already have travel insurance?
Yes — standard travel insurance typically does not cover the unique risks associated with cruising. You need to either add a cruise extension to your existing policy or purchase dedicated cruise insurance. Standard policies often exclude cabin confinement, miss-port departure, and have inadequate limits for maritime medical evacuations.
Does my NZ health insurance cover me on a cruise?
No. Once your ship departs port — even on domestic NZ cruises — you are in a maritime environment where standard NZ health insurance does not apply. Ship's doctors charge private rates, and onboard medical costs are entirely your responsibility without cruise insurance.
What is cabin confinement cover?
Cabin confinement cover provides a daily cash benefit (typically $100–$250/day) if illness or injury keeps you confined to your cabin. Since you've paid for excursions and activities you can't enjoy, this benefit helps compensate for the experience you're missing out on.
What is missed port departure cover?
If your connecting flight or transport to the cruise embarkation port is delayed or cancelled due to a covered reason, missed port departure cover pays for you to catch up with your ship at the next port — including flights, accommodation, and meals while you wait.
