Comprehensive Cruise Insurance — Full Cover Explained

Full-coverage cruise insurance with all protection options

Comprehensive cruise insurance is your complete protection against everything that could go wrong before or during your cruise. Unlike basic policies covering only medical emergencies, comprehensive insurance includes trip cancellation, medical coverage, emergency evacuation, baggage protection, cruise line default, and more. Our guide explains what comprehensive cover includes, why it matters, and how to find the best comprehensive cruise insurance policies in New Zealand.

Why It Matters

Understanding your cruise insurance options helps you make the best choice for your holiday.

All-Inclusive Coverage

Comprehensive cruise insurance combines medical protection, trip cancellation, emergency evacuation, baggage loss, travel delays, and cruise line default protection into one policy. You're covered against virtually any event that could disrupt your cruise. This is the highest level of protection available.

Trip Cancellation Protection

Cancel for covered reasons (illness, injury, family emergency) and your entire cruise fare is refunded, plus associated costs like flights and accommodation. Comprehensive policies reimburse your full investment if you can't travel.

Medical & Evacuation Cover

Full medical protection covers doctor visits, hospital treatment, medications, and emergency evacuation anywhere in the world. If you're seriously ill at sea, evacuation to proper medical facilities is covered—sometimes costing $100,000+ without insurance.

Baggage & Travel Protection

Comprehensive cover includes lost or delayed baggage reimbursement, travel delay coverage if you miss the ship, and sometimes cruise line default protection if the cruise line fails. You're protected for delays, lost luggage, and unexpected disruptions.

Common Questions

Get answers to the most frequently asked questions about cruise insurance.

What does comprehensive cruise insurance cover that basic policies don't?

Comprehensive cruise insurance includes everything in basic policies (medical, evacuation) plus trip cancellation, baggage protection, travel delay cover, and cruise line default protection. Basic policies cover medical emergencies; comprehensive policies cover cancellation if you can't travel, baggage if luggage is lost, delays getting to the ship, and the cruise line going out of business. Comprehensive is significantly broader protection.

Is comprehensive cruise insurance worth the extra cost?

For most cruisers, comprehensive insurance is worth the extra investment. Trip cancellation and baggage coverage alone provide valuable protection—cruises are expensive, and losing a fare is devastating without insurance. If you've invested significantly in your cruise, comprehensive cover protects your entire investment for a modest premium increase.

What's the difference between comprehensive and premium comprehensive?

Premium comprehensive policies offer higher benefit limits, wider coverage terms, and sometimes extras like travel inconvenience cover or onboard credits. Standard comprehensive policies cover the essentials; premium versions extend limits and coverage. Compare options to see which level suits your cruise and budget.
