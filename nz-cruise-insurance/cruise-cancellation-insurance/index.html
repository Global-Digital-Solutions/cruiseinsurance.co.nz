Cruise Cancellation Insurance — Protect Your Fare

Trip cancellation cover for NZ cruise holidays

Cruise fares can be expensive, and cancelling a cruise can result in significant financial loss if you're not insured. Cruise cancellation insurance reimburses your cruise fare and associated costs if you need to cancel for covered reasons—illness, injury, family bereavement, redundancy, or other qualifying events. Our guide explains what cancellation insurance covers, why it matters, and how to get it in New Zealand.

Why It Matters

Understanding your cruise insurance options helps you make the best choice for your holiday.

What Cancellation Cover Protects

Cruise cancellation insurance reimburses your fare if you cancel before departure due to covered reasons like sudden illness, injury, family death, or unforeseen emergency. The policy pays out your cruise fare, pre-booked flights, accommodation, and excursions—protecting your full holiday investment.

Covered Cancellation Reasons

Legitimate covered reasons typically include unexpected illness or injury to you or a family member, death in the family, redundancy, jury duty, or being called back for emergency work. Check your policy details for the full list. Pre-existing conditions have specific rules—declare them when applying.

Maximum Benefit Limits

Cancellation policies reimburse up to the insured amount—typically your cruise fare plus flights and accommodation. Know your policy's maximum benefit. Expensive cruises should be insured for the full fare. Some policies allow you to choose your benefit level.

Purchase Before Booking

Cancellation cover is most effective when purchased before or very soon after you book your cruise. Buying cancellation insurance after you book gives you the most complete protection. Purchasing very close to departure may limit cancellation cover availability.

Common Questions

Get answers to the most frequently asked questions about cruise insurance.

What cancellation reasons are covered by cruise insurance in NZ?

Standard cancellation coverage typically includes unexpected illness or injury, death in the family, redundancy, unforeseen emergencies, and sometimes cancellation by the cruise line. Pre-existing medical conditions are usually only covered if declared and accepted. Always read your policy details—coverage varies between insurers. Some policies exclude cancellation due to travel warnings or pandemics unless specifically added.

If I cancel my cruise, will I get the full fare back?

Cancellation insurance reimburses the amount you're entitled to claim under the policy. This typically covers your cruise fare minus any refund from the cruise line, plus pre-booked flights, accommodation, and excursions up to the policy limit. You won't profit from cancellation—insurance reimburses your actual losses. Check your policy's benefit limit and what's included.

When should I buy cruise cancellation insurance?

Purchase cancellation insurance at the same time you book your cruise or shortly after, while it's still optional. Buying it early ensures maximum coverage for any issues that might come up before your departure date. Most cancellation cover becomes unavailable once your cruise is within 7–14 days of departure. Waiting until last-minute means losing this crucial protection.
