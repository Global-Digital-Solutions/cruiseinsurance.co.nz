Cruise Medical Insurance — Medical Coverage That Covers Everything

Emergency medical coverage for NZ cruise travellers

Medical emergencies while cruising can be devastating without proper insurance. Cruise medical insurance covers emergency doctor visits, hospital treatment, medications, and evacuation to proper medical facilities—essential protection when you're far from home at sea. Our guide explains what medical coverage includes, why it's crucial for cruising, and how to ensure you have adequate medical protection for your holiday.

Why It Matters

Understanding your cruise insurance options helps you make the best choice for your holiday.

Doctor Visits & Hospital Care

Cruise medical insurance covers consultations with ship's doctors and shore-based medical facilities. If you need hospital treatment due to illness or injury, your policy pays for admission, procedures, medication, and treatment. Medical costs overseas can be thousands of dollars—insurance protects you from devastating bills.

Emergency Evacuation

If you have a serious medical emergency at sea, evacuation to the nearest proper medical facility is covered. Air evacuation can cost $50,000–$200,000+ without insurance. Medical cruise insurance covers helicopter or plane evacuation, transportation to hospitals, and emergency stabilisation.

Prescription Medications & Dentistry

Medical cover includes prescriptions for medications you need while cruising, plus emergency dental treatment for pain or infection. Having medication coverage is important if you take regular prescription drugs and need refills while on holiday.

Ongoing Treatment & Chronic Conditions

If you have a chronic medical condition, quality cruise medical insurance covers treatment for that condition while cruising (if declared and accepted). This includes medication management, ongoing care, and emergency treatment related to your condition.

Common Questions

Get answers to the most frequently asked questions about cruise insurance.

What exactly does cruise medical insurance cover?

Cruise medical insurance covers emergency medical treatment caused by sudden illness or injury while cruising. This includes doctor visits (ship and shore), hospital stays, surgery, medications, diagnostic tests, emergency dental care, and evacuation. The policy pays for treatment overseas and transportation to adequate medical facilities. It doesn't cover routine care or pre-planned treatment.

Does medical cover include evacuation insurance?

Quality cruise medical insurance includes emergency medical evacuation. If you're seriously ill or injured at sea and need transportation to proper medical facilities, evacuation is covered. This can cost tens of thousands of dollars without insurance. Check your policy includes evacuation—it's essential for cruise holidays.

Are pre-existing medical conditions covered?

Many cruise medical insurance policies cover pre-existing conditions if they're stable and declared when you apply. Some insurers automatically cover conditions if you're receiving ongoing treatment. Always declare existing conditions—if not disclosed, claims may be rejected. Ask about pre-existing condition terms when getting your quote.
