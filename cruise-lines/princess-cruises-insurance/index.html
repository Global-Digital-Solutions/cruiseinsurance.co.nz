Why Princess Captivates Kiwi Travellers

Princess Cruises has won hearts across Aotearoa. The line is synonymous with elegance, exceptional dining, and intimate itineraries. Kiwis especially love Princess for milestone moments: honeymoons in the Mediterranean departing from Sydney, golden anniversary cruises through New Zealand's fiords, or bucket-list round-the-world voyages. The ships are smaller than Royal Caribbean's megaships—there's an exclusive feel despite sailing with 3,500 passengers.

Princess also offers what few competitors do: regular NZ/Pacific departures plus easy connections to international itineraries. Want to start in Auckland and sail the South Pacific? Princess does that. Want to connect to a Europe-bound voyage? Also possible.

The Insurance Complexity: Romance, Distance & Expense

Princess passengers book differently. They're celebrating milestones, planning once-in-a-lifetime trips, and spending premium money. A honeymoon cancellation hits differently than a family cruise cancellation.

Extended voyages (20–90+ days) introduce new dynamics: crew changes, longer medical response times, and multi-country complications if evacuation is needed. A wedding on the ship, shore excursions in remote locations, and onboard enrichment classes all create insurable moments.

The catch? High passenger spend means high cancellation values. Your policy must protect the full booking value—often NZ$8,000–$20,000.

Typical NZ Itineraries

  • **NZ & Pacific**: 10–14 day sailings from Auckland (Fiji, Samoa, Tonga, Bay of Islands)
  • **Australia East Coast**: 7–12 day Sydney-based or fly-cruise itineraries
  • **International cruising**: 14–21 day Asia-Pacific crossings; repositioning cruises to Europe/Americas
  • **Extended voyages**: 45–90+ day world cruises departing Sydney (once or twice annually)

Insurance Must-Haves

**High-value trip cancellation**: Princess passengers spend big. Ensure your cover reflects the actual cruise fare (NZ$8,000–$15,000+), not a generic NZ$3,000 limit.

**Shore excursion insurance**: Princess encourages adventurous off-ship activities—hiking Milford Sound, swimming in Fiji, wine-tasting in Australian wine regions. Many standard policies exclude these. Upgrade to shore excursion coverage.

**Extended departure windows**: Standard policies might only refund if you cancel 7–14 days before departure. Princess cruises are booked 6–18 months ahead. Request coverage that protects you up to the day before departure.

**Wedding/honeymoon clauses**: If celebrating a milestone, ensure your policy explicitly covers wedding-related cancellations and has spousal/partner carve-outs.

**Medical evacuation with international reach**: Princess itineraries span the Pacific and beyond. Your policy must cover helicopter evacuation from Fiji, sea rescue from remote waters, and medical repatriation to NZ.*
